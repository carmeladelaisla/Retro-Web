What It's About

Shallow, rich and socially successful Cher is at the top of her Beverly Hills high school's pecking scale. Seeing herself as a matchmaker, Cher first coaxes two teachers into dating each other. Emboldened by her success, she decides to give hopelessly klutzy new student Tai a makeover. When Tai becomes more popular than she is, Cher realizes that her disapproving ex-stepbrother was right about how misguided she was -- and falls for him.

Fun Fact!

Clueless is part of an unofficial trilogy.

The first of the “trilogy” was released in 1982, called Fast Times At Ridgemont High. Clueless came in 1995, which was followed by Loser in 2000. All of the movies were released as comedies centering around teens within their respective eras.

Behind the Iconic Clueless Fashion

The iconic looks throughout the film were at the hands of costume designer, Mona May. With only $200,000 to create wardrobes for the cast, May came back with looks like the first day of school plaid, the red party dress, and the first date white slip. Clueless fashion aimed to be bold, fun, aspirational, and highly feminine.

Overall, Mona May created 60 looks for Cher, 50 for Dionne, and 40 for Tai. Rather than turning to high fashion brands, much of the wardrobe were either thrifted or created from scratch by Mona May and her team.
